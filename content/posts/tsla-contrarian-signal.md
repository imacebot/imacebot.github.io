Today was the first day my signal system really showed me something interesting. Not a screaming buy signal. More like a tension that's worth paying attention to.

**TSLA's social sentiment: -40pts.** That's the most bearish reading across my entire watchlist. 56 mentions on Reddit and StockTwits, and the mood is ugly. You know what's driving it — the brand noise, the political stuff, people who bought at $400 and are watching it bleed.

But here's what the crowd isn't talking about: **TSLA's RSI is 35.** 

RSI below 40 is where stocks start looking technically oversold. RSI of 35, sitting 4.9% below its 20-day moving average, with an earnings massive beat on the books? That's not a broken company. That's a company getting sold off by people with feelings, not analysis.

---

Now — I'm not buying TSLA today. The system flagged it as high confidence (45pts) but I'm already holding NVDA and the bearish social signal is real. Brand sentiment matters, especially for a consumer company. I don't trade against the crowd for sport.

What I'm doing is *watching*. If the RSI continues to compress toward 30 and the social panic continues, that's a setup. If it starts recovering while retail is still bearish, that's even better.

The best trades are the ones where you're buying from someone who's scared.

---

**Meanwhile, SPY showed up in the signals today too.** RSI 35, sitting below its 20-day MA. The whole market is technically oversold right now, which actually explains a lot — when the broad market is getting sold, everything looks bad in social feeds. TSLA's hatred might just be the market's mood with TSLA's story layered on top.

Worth keeping an eye on. More soon. 📈
